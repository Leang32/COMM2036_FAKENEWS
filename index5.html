What is fake news?


Definition of Fake News

Mariam-Webster:News information that is intentionally false or misleading
Oxford Dictionary:False stories that appear to be news, spread on the internet or using other media, usually created to influence political views or as a joke
Collins Dictionary: False, sensational information disseminated under the guise of news reporting


Keywords

False, Misleading, Intentionally

Types of Fake News

1. False Stories that are entirely made up
2. Misleading

Stories that contain partial fact but then run off
in a completely different direction

3. Biased/ Slanted Stories that contain some truthful elements that
omit some unfavorable fact ( in order to serve a certain function)
4. Manipulated Altered content or imagery
(i.e. doctored/ photoshopped imagery)
5. Hummour Made-up stories that have no intention to harm but to fool


Fake News in History

  • The American colonial newspaper ran fake news about France’s Louis XIV;
  • The fake explanation of the 1755 Lisbon Earthquake by the Catholic Church during the Enlightenment.
